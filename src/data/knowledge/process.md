# How a Project With Me Actually Goes

Every project I take on is a partnership, not a transaction. Here's how that usually plays out, start to finish.

## 1. First call (30 minutes)
We start with about the first 10 minutes getting to know each other — that's where I figure out if we're a good fit, both ways. I'm looking for clients who treat the partnership with mutual respect: you bring the vision and the deep knowledge of your business; I bring the technical expertise. The clients I do my best work with are clear about what they want and trust me to recommend the right way to build it. If you'd rather direct every implementation detail yourself, I'm probably not your guy — and that's okay.

If we click, we'll spend the rest of the call talking through your actual problem, and I'll share some initial directions for how I'd think about solving it.

## 2. Conversation summary
After the call, I'll send you a written summary of what we talked about — your problem, the directions I'd consider, and any open questions. This gives you breathing room to think through whether you want to move forward, without any pressure on the call itself.

## 3. SOW (if we both want to move forward)
If we both decide we're a fit, I'll draft a Statement of Work with the scope, deliverables, timeline, and pricing. No surprises — everything is in writing before any work or money changes hands.

## 4. Payment & kickoff
- **Methods:** ACH or check
- **Structure:** 50% up front, 50% on completion
- **Retainers:** Most projects also include a monthly retainer for ongoing support and improvements. Some smaller, well-bounded projects are one-and-done — we'll talk through what makes sense for yours.

Once the deposit clears, you'll get an invite link to my client portal (CRM). You'll set up your profile, submit support tickets there, and see real-time build progress.

## 5. Build phase
I work in a staging environment from day one, so you can poke at the work as it grows — no waiting until the end to see what you're getting. We'll have regular check-ins, and you can submit feedback or questions through the portal anytime.

## 6. Launch
Launches are planned, not rushed. We'll coordinate the date so you can prepare any marketing or customer communication around it. The goal is for launch day to feel like a non-event because everything's already been tested and you've already seen it working.

## 7. After launch
For most clients, the monthly retainer covers ongoing support, small improvements, and the next thing you didn't know you needed yet. For one-and-done projects, I make sure you walk away with everything you need to keep running on your own.

## What's different about working with me
I'm not building you a generic SaaS knockoff. I'm building a custom application from the ground up — yours, owned by you, built for the way *you* actually run your business. The point is something you can use for the foreseeable future, not something you'll outgrow in six months when the SaaS pricing changes.
