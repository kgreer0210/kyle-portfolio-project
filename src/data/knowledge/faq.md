# Frequently Asked Questions

## Are you available right now? How booked are you?
I take on a small number of projects at a time so I can give each one real attention. Depending on the month, I may have immediate availability or a short waitlist. The 30-minute call is the best way to find out.

## Do you do this full-time?
No — I'm a full-time software developer at Robins Financial Credit Union, and KYGR Solutions is the work I take on in my evenings and weekends, for projects I'm genuinely excited about. I'm honest about timelines because of that. Down the road, depending on how interest in my services grows, taking this full-time is something I'd be open to.

## Where are you located? Do you only work with local clients?
I'm based in Warner Robins, Georgia. Most of my work has been with local businesses in middle Georgia, but I'm fully open to remote clients across the U.S.

## How much does a project cost?
Pricing depends entirely on scope, so I don't quote ranges without a real conversation. After our first call, you'll get a written SOW with everything spelled out — no surprises.

## How long does a project take?
Most projects land somewhere between a few weeks and a few months, depending on complexity. Because I'm doing KYGR work part-time around a full-time job, I'm honest about timelines up front and won't promise something I can't deliver.

## Do I own the code and what I'm paying for?
Yes. You own the application, the code, the data — all of it. I'm not building a SaaS you rent from me; I'm building something that's yours.

## Can you work with my existing tools (CRM, website, payment system, etc.)?
Often, yes — most projects involve some integration with existing tools. That said, I'll be upfront: integration complexity varies a lot depending on the system. I'd rather talk it through on the first call so I can be honest about what's straightforward and what would take more discovery. I won't pretend I've integrated with something I haven't.

## What if I'm not technical at all?
That's the most common situation, and honestly, it's part of why I do this. You don't need to know the tech — you just need to know your business. I handle the rest.

## Are you a Christian developer? Do you only work with Christian businesses?
Yes, I'm a Christian, and following Christ shapes how I work — but I work with all kinds of clients, regardless of faith background. In practice, what that means for you is: I'll be honest about what I can and can't do, I won't oversell features you don't need, and I'll tell you the truth even when it's not the easy answer. I don't expect my clients to share my faith — most don't, and that's totally fine. The only projects I'll politely turn down are ones that would directly conflict with my convictions, and if that ever comes up I'll be upfront about it early so we don't waste each other's time.

## What's your tech stack?
Mostly React and Next.js on the front end, with whatever backend makes sense for the project — that could be a serverless stack like Convex, a traditional Node/Express setup, or something else depending on what you need. For AI features I've worked with OpenAI, OpenRouter, and Retell. For mobile I use React Native. I pick the right tool for the job, not the same stack every time.

## Do you do maintenance and support after launch?
Yes — most clients stay on a monthly retainer that covers ongoing support, small improvements, and the next thing they didn't know they needed yet. Some smaller, well-bounded projects are one-and-done, and I'll be upfront about which bucket yours falls into. Either way, you won't be left hanging after launch day.

## Do you work with solo entrepreneurs or very small businesses?
That's actually most of my clients. You don't need a big team or a fully formed idea — just a real problem you're trying to solve. Some of the best projects I've worked on started with "I have this thing I keep doing manually and I hate it."

## What happens on the first call?
It's about 30 minutes. The first part is us getting to know each other — I want to make sure we're a good fit on both sides before anything else. If we click, we'll spend the rest of the call on your actual problem, and I'll share some initial thoughts on how I'd approach it. No pitch, no pressure to commit. After the call I'll send you a written summary of what we covered.

## What do you need from me to get started?
Not much — just a clear sense of the problem you're trying to solve. You don't need a spec, a wireframe, or any technical knowledge. Come to the first call with: what's hurting right now, what you've tried so far (even if nothing), and roughly what a good outcome looks like. We handle everything else from there.
